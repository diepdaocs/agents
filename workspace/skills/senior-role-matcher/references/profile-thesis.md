# Profile thesis

## Core summary
- Singapore-based senior software engineer with 10+ years across backend engineering, data engineering, ML/AI, fintech, and some blockchain work
- Strongest languages: Java and Python
- Production experience in financial markets, risk/trading applications, NLP/ML platforms, data pipelines, and internal/platform systems

## Strongest story angles

### 1. Finance / trading systems engineer
Use for banks, exchanges, trading firms, market-data firms, risk-tech teams, and electronic trading platforms.

Evidence:
- Built financial systems and risk/trading-adjacent software
- Strong Java/Python foundation fits enterprise finance and trading-tech hiring
- Credible with real-time, backend, and reliability-heavy environments

### 2. Backend / platform engineer
Use for senior backend, distributed systems, platform engineering, and infrastructure roles.

Evidence:
- Broad production backend background
- Experience across data-intensive and integration-heavy systems
- Strong fit for internal platforms and engineering-heavy products

### 3. Data / ML platform engineer
Use for ML infrastructure, NLP platforms, data platforms, AI engineering, and applied-ML delivery roles.

Evidence:
- Worked on NLP/ML platforms and data pipelines
- Strong fit for engineering delivery around AI/ML rather than pure research
- Comfortable spanning productionization, data flow, and platform concerns

### 4. Blockchain as differentiator
Use as a secondary angle when relevant, not the default headline.

Evidence:
- Some blockchain background broadens the addressable market
- Best used when a role sits at the intersection of finance + infrastructure + digital assets

## Positioning rules
- Lead with finance/trading when the employer is a bank, trading firm, exchange, or market-infrastructure company.
- Lead with backend/platform when the role is broad and infrastructure-heavy.
- Lead with data/ML platform when the role is clearly AI or data platform oriented.
- Mention blockchain only when it increases credibility; do not let it crowd out stronger signals.
